Community Results So Far
What Respondents Have Experienced

--% of respondents have visited San Antonio landmarks like Market Square, the Arneson River Theatre, and attended Fiesta events — places and traditions woven into the cultural fabric of this city.

What Most Never Knew

Yet --% were unaware of the man who spent over two decades building cultural pride in this city, sustaining folklórico enrollment of young men and women, including at risk youth, at a time when most programs could not.

The Name Behind the Legacy

And --% have never heard the name Porfirio "Pete" Ramirez.

Market Square, the Arneson River Theatre, and the spirit of Fiesta in San Antonio all carry a piece of Pete Ramirez's legacy.

He built cultural pride and confidence in every student who walked through his door, young men and women who needed somewhere to belong and something to be proud of. In a world where only 8% of boys choose dance at all, he kept them coming back, year after year, for over two decades.

He performed 150 to 200 shows a year. He toured Japan. He made national news in Life Magazine. A plaque dedicated in his honor hangs at the Arneson River Theatre.

And most San Antonians have never heard his name. That is why this project exists.
